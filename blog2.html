trying new things [09.26.2024]

Ten days ago, I wrote about new beginnings. Today, I'm writing about the terrifying, exhilarating process of actually following through.

I spent this week learning Rust. Not because I need it for work—my day-to-day is mostly Python and JavaScript—but because I wanted to understand memory management at a deeper level. There's something humbling about going from feeling competent to feeling like a complete beginner again. Every error message is a puzzle, every successful compilation feels like magic.

The thing about trying new things is that it forces you to confront your own limitations. I thought I understood programming concepts until I had to think about ownership and borrowing. It's like learning to drive in a country where the rules of the road are completely different—you have to unlearn instincts that have become second nature.

At Verizon, we're experimenting with some new AI frameworks that just came out. The team lead asked if anyone wanted to take point on researching them. Six months ago, I would have waited for someone else to volunteer. This time, I raised my hand. The learning curve has been steep, but there's something addictive about that feeling of your brain stretching to accommodate new concepts.

Photography side note: I tried shooting street photography for the first time this weekend. The anxiety of pointing a camera at strangers is real, but I captured this moment of an elderly man feeding pigeons that I'm actually proud of. Sometimes the best shots happen when you're willing to be uncomfortable.

The common thread in all of this is that growth doesn't happen in the planning phase. It happens when you're willing to look foolish, to ask stupid questions, to build something that might not work. The comfort zone is a beautiful place, but nothing grows there.

Next week: maybe I'll finally try that Japanese cooking class. Or maybe I'll keep pushing on this Rust project until it actually does something useful. The point isn't the specific thing—it's the practice of choosing curiosity over comfort, over and over again.
